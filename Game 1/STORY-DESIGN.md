## üéØ CORE DESIGN PRINCIPLES

### Priority: Puzzles > Story
Based on user research (50 people surveyed):
- **Players prefer**: Puzzles, creative problem-solving, escape room mechanics
- **Story is**: A "rail" connecting puzzles, not the main attraction
- **Remember**: Like escape rooms - people remember fun puzzles, not deep lore

### Text-Based Game Strengths
‚úÖ **What works:**
- Thorough investigation of contained locations
- Container puzzles (unlock, open, search creatively)
- Item finding and combination (max 3 items)
- Creative NPC interactions (distract, bribe, trade)
- Forensic gameplay (fingerprints, photos, analysis)

‚ùå **What doesn't work:**
- Time pressure/urgency (no real-time in text)
- Chase sequences/running
- Stealth mechanics
- Action sequences

### Anti-Frustration Goals
üö´ **AVOID:**
- Repetitive mechanics (same lock type every chapter)
- Repetitive procedures (same goal structure)
- Repetitive commands (too many identical puzzles)
- Comparing timestamps/dates (doesn't work in games)
- Reading long documents/papers (boring)

‚úÖ **ENSURE:**
- Puzzle variety across chapters
- Different container types per location
- Creative solutions (AI judges player attempts)
- Escalating difficulty (Chapter 1 easier than Chapter 5)
- Different goal types per chapter

## üéÆ CHAPTERS 1-5 STRUCTURE

### Design Philosophy

**Each Chapter:**
- ‚úÖ Different location (completely new setting)
- ‚úÖ Different goal type (avoid repetition)
- ‚úÖ Different media from Sarah (PDF ‚Üí Photo ‚Üí Audio ‚Üí Video ‚Üí Encrypted)
- ‚úÖ Different puzzle focus (locks ‚Üí forensics ‚Üí logic ‚Üí spatial, etc.)
- ‚úÖ Different container types (themed to location)
- ‚úÖ ~90-120 minutes playtime

**Story is minimal** - just connects puzzle locations. Players discover goals organically through:
- Sarah's messages (hints at what's needed)
- In-game context (examining documents, reading notes)
- NPC dialogue (clues about what to look for)

### Chapter Flow Pattern

```
1. OPENING: Sarah sends message with media
   ‚Üì
2. GOAL EMERGES: Player reads/views media, understands task
   ‚Üì
3. INVESTIGATE: Explore location, solve puzzles
   ‚Üì
4. COMPLETION: Contact Sarah, send findings
   ‚Üì
5. TRANSITION: Sarah responds, sets up next chapter
```

**Example - Chapter 1 Puzzle Chain**:
- Player finds CCTV Screenshot showing license plate **LKJ-9472** (Evidence 1)
- Player notices numeric portion **9472** might be useful
- Player discovers Garage #3 has digital keypad requiring 4-digit PIN
- Player enters **9472** ‚Üí Garage opens
- Inside: Van registration card showing "Jeremy Miller, 447 Willow Lane" (Evidence 2)
- Address **447 Willow Lane** sets up Chapter 3 investigation (Bloom Estate)

**Location**: Elm Street (9 zones - crime scene area)

**Opening Message** (Sarah Chen):

- Goal emerges organically: Find the gray van and who operates it

- Send to Sarah via phone

- Physical items (registration cards, receipts)
- Photos (use phone camera - CCTV screenshots)
- Audio recordings (use phone to record witness statements)

- **Evidence 2** (HARD): License plate number provides PIN code **9472** to unlock Garage #3
- **Evidence 2 Contents**: Van registration showing "Jeremy Miller, 447 Willow Lane"
- **Story Connection**: Address (447 Willow Lane) leads to Chapter 3 investigation (Bloom Estate)

## üé® DESIGN GUIDELINES

### Container Variety

**Each chapter uses different container types themed to location**:

- **Chapter 1 (Elm Street)**: Dumpsters, truck toolboxes, filing cabinets, garages, benches
- **Chapter 2 (Greenhouse)**: Planters, seed boxes, irrigation panels, storage sheds
- **Chapter 3 (Archive)**: Evidence boxes, filing cabinets, locked drawers, microfiche
- **Chapter 4 (Jazz Club)**: Instrument cases, bar storage, dressing room trunks, stage props
- **Chapter 5 (Estate)**: Safes, vaults, antique furniture, family heirlooms

### Unlock Mechanisms (Vary Each Chapter)

**Chapter 1 Examples**:
- Lock pick minigame
- Digital keypad (embedded game)
- NPC distraction (AI judges)
- Multi-part tool assembly
- Combination lock (riddle-based)
- Secret door (puzzle mechanism)

**Future Chapters**: Different mechanisms, no repetition

### NPC Design

**Type 1 (Useful)**:
- Has crucial item or information
- Blocks access initially
- Unlocks via: Trade, distract, solve problem, bribe
- After helping: Converts to Type 2 (flavor dialogue)

**Type 2 (Flavor)**:
- Atmosphere, world-building
- General talk
- May give hints or red herrings
- No crucial items

**AI-Judged Creativity**:
- Electrician distraction example
- Player tries creative solutions
- AI agent judges: Good ‚Üí NPC distracted, Bad ‚Üí try again
- Reusable (player can retry)

### Tool Placement

**Rule**: Tools found 1-2 zones away from primary use
- Promotes purposeful backtracking
- Creates "aha!" moments
- Avoids frustration (not 3+ zones away)

**Examples**:
- Crowbar in Zone 2 ‚Üí Use in Zone 5
- Wire cutters in Zone 2 ‚Üí Use in Zone 7
- Drill parts scattered: Zone 2, 5, 7 ‚Üí Assemble ‚Üí Use in Zone 9
